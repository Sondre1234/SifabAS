## Project Background

Bay du Nord is a deepwater oil development in the Flemish Pass basin, approximately 500 km northeast of St. John's, Newfoundland and Labrador, Canada. The project was originally sanctioned by Equinor in 2022 but was subsequently reshaped ("Core BdN") to optimize development costs while maintaining production targets.

## Scope of Supply

### Sifab Opportunity: Flow Measurement Package

- **Estimated Value:** $2.12 million USD
- **Expected Award:** 2027 (post-FID, during EPC phase)
- **Scope:** V-Cone differential pressure flow meters and associated flow measurement instrumentation for the FPSO

### Sifab's Value Proposition

- **V-Cone Meters:** Sifab is an authorized channel partner for McCrometer, manufacturer of the V-Cone meter -- a specialized differential pressure flow element with advantages in space-constrained FPSO applications (no straight-run requirements, low permanent pressure loss, wide turndown ratio)
- **Norway Presence:** Sifab's location in Sandnes provides proximity to Equinor's headquarters in Stavanger and BW Offshore's office in Oslo, enabling direct engagement during FEED and EPC
- **Offshore Track Record:** Proven experience with Norsok-compliant skid fabrication and flow metering solutions for North Sea operators

## Key Standards

This is a Canadian offshore project. The applicable standards framework differs from typical North Sea (Norsok) projects:

- [ ] ANSI/NEMA (primary electrical/instrumentation standards)
- [ ] ASME standards (pressure equipment, piping)
- [ ] API standards (petroleum industry)
- [ ] CSA standards (Canadian Standards Association -- may apply)
- [ ] Equinor Technical Requirements (TR) -- to be confirmed during FEED
- [ ] Client-specific specifications (BW Offshore / Equinor)

> **Note:** Norsok standards are NOT the primary standard basis for this project. However, Equinor may require certain Norsok-aligned practices. Confirm during FEED engagement.

## Notes

- This is a high-value opportunity ($2.12M USD) but with a long timeline. FID is not expected until late 2026, and the flow measurement package award is likely in 2027.
- The FPSO will be built by BW Offshore, who is currently running FEED from their Oslo office. Early vendor qualification and engagement with BW Offshore is important.
- V-Cone meters are well-suited for FPSO applications due to their compact design (no upstream/downstream straight-run requirements), which is a significant advantage in space-constrained topsides.
- Standards basis is ANSI/NEMA, not Norsok. This is a key difference from Sifab's typical North Sea work. McCrometer's involvement will be critical for standards compliance.
