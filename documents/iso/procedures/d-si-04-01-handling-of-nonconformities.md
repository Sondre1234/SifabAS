## Purpose

This procedure describes how Sifab AS reports and follows up Nonconformity/RUH/Improvement reports and nonconforming products. The purpose of nonconformity reporting and treatment is to reach solutions that improve product and service quality, and to prevent harm to people, material and the external environment.

## Definitions

- **Nonconformity (Avvik)** — a product or service that does not meet specified requirements.
- **RUH** — Report of an unwanted incident: injuries, near-misses, accidents and similar events.
- **Improvement / improvement potential** — anything observed that could be done better regarding safety, quality, economy, time-saving or otherwise; reported through the same system as nonconformities.

## Scope

This procedure applies to all Nonconformity/RUH/Improvement reports filed at Sifab AS, logged in the **Deviations - NCR Tracker**.

## Responsibility

The General Manager is responsible for ensuring this procedure is implemented. Everyone is required to file a report when something unwanted happens or an improvement potential is identified, and everyone is required to participate in the improvement process.

> To succeed at improvement, Sifab needs visibility of as many unwanted incidents as possible — no matter how small. Filing a report is never an accusation, and no one at Sifab AS will receive a negative response from management for a report they file in good faith.

## Description

### Nonconforming Products — Immediate Action

When a nonconformity is identified on a product, the person who identifies it immediately notifies the Project Manager, who determines further action. If a product is reworked or repaired, it shall always be re-inspected per the quality plan or other document describing the required testing.

### Reporting a Nonconformity

The report is filed using the NCR form (**D.SI.04-F01**) and logged in the **Deviations - NCR Tracker / Open** folder. Sifab AS has retired Zigma360 for deviation tracking (2026-07-13); the NCR Register (**D.SI.04-R01**) is now the sole system of record. New deviations use the format **NCR-YYYY-NNN** (e.g. NCR-2026-001), resetting to 001 each January — see the register for the next available number. The report must describe the nonconformity clearly enough to unambiguously trace it to the affected product.

### RUH and Immediate Action

Any condition that could endanger people, material or the environment is corrected immediately. If such conditions recur, this is reported to the General Manager through the nonconformity system, and verbally if appropriate, so correct action can be taken. All accidents and near-misses are reported through the same system, as are equipment, tooling and electrical faults.

### Treatment of Reports

The General Manager receives all reports and may delegate further treatment. Treatment is carried out at the Sifab AS Monday meeting and logged in the Deviations - NCR Tracker.

Treatment consists of identifying root causes and assessing the consequences of the event, done where practical together with those involved. All findings are recorded and form the basis for decisions taken; in some cases, action beyond any immediate fix is needed to prevent recurrence (corrective action, **D.SI.04.02**).

Corrective action should be implemented when:
- A nonconformity/unwanted event of a given type recurs.
- A nonconformity/unwanted event is significant (high consequence).

Corrective actions that cannot be implemented immediately are entered into an action plan. Whoever treats the report always gives feedback to the person who filed it on the decision taken; if action is delayed or not taken, this is explained to the person who filed the report.

### Filing and Follow-Up

All reports are retained for follow-up, including trend analysis used by management in the continual-improvement process (management review, **A.SI.01.01 §7.4**).

## HSE Considerations

Nonconformity treatment should be carried out in a factual, non-blaming tone.
