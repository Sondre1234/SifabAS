## 1. Purpose

This document maps Sifab AS's core processes in sequence, identifying for each: the responsible role, objective, inputs, outputs, risks and opportunities, per ISO 9001:2015 §4.4 and §6.1.

## 2. Process Map

| Process | Responsible | Objective | Input | Output | Risk | Opportunity |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **D.SI.02.01** Procedure for Quotation | Sales Manager | Ensure a thorough bidding process | Inquiry / need from client | A quality-controlled, correct bid | Bid issued without expected quality control | Quality-checking bids reduces the risk of mistakes |
| **D.SI.01.03** Evaluation and Approval of Suppliers | Project Manager / General Manager | Maintain an updated list of approved Sifab suppliers | Prospective supplier | Approved and checked supplier | Poor supplier quality damages Sifab's reputation | Satisfied customers purchase more from Sifab |
| **D.SI.02.02** Contract Review | Project Manager / General Manager | Confirm all items to be purchased are correct and match expectations and plan | Inquiry / purchase order | Quality-checked and accepted bid / purchase order | Potential financial loss; delivering products out of spec | Double-checking/verifying orders and bids reduces financial and product risk |
| **D.SI.01.01** Procedure for Purchasing | Project Manager / General Manager | Maintain company purchasing objectives economically, and ensure the purchaser follows an appropriate system | Order and specifications from client | Correct purchase order to supplier | Mistakes can lead to wrong products; product might not be accepted by client | Register and evaluate deviations to secure good quality and communication through the project and delivery phase |
| **D.SI.02.03** Procedure for Order Processing | Project Manager | Ensure thorough and correct order processing | Purchase order, specifications/requirements | Correct product including documentation | Incomplete or incorrect order handling | Consistent order processing supports on-time, correct delivery |
| **C.SI.01.01** Procedure for Design and Development | Project Manager | Ensure design work purchased from sub-suppliers meets customer requirements (§8.3 permitted exclusion — design itself is subcontracted) | Customer requirement specification | Verified and validated design ready for fabrication by the sub-supplier | Design fault not caught before fabrication starts, discovered late and expensive to fix | Structured design review of the sub-supplier's output catches issues early and protects the delivery schedule |
| **D.SI.07.01** Procedure for Job Safety Analysis (SJA) | Project Manager | Ensure a safe and controlled work process | SJA form | Completed SJA form | Unconsidered risk can lead to incidents | Safe and controlled working environment |
| **B.SI.01.01** Procedure for Incoming Goods | Project Manager | Ensure goods are checked on receipt and stored acceptably | Goods from supplier | Approved goods | Rejected product; transport damage | Claim to supplier; documented problem; insurance claim where applicable |
| **B.SI.01.02** Procedure for Identification and Traceability | Project Manager / Workshop | Keep materials and components traceable from receipt through fabrication to delivery | Material certificates, batch/heat/serial data | Marked, traceable components with retained certificates | Loss of traceability prevents root-cause tracing after delivery, and is a safety issue for pressure-retaining parts | Full traceability supports fast, credible response to any field issue and demonstrates NORSOK/PED compliance |
| **B.SI.01.03** Procedure for Control and Testing | Project Manager | Document/prove that products fabricated or supplied by Sifab are checked and verified against specifications and requirements | Client testing / calibration requirements | Approved product | Not delivering as promised | Verified conformance builds customer confidence |
| **B.SI.01.04** Procedure for Handling, Storage, Packing, Protection and Shipment | Project Manager | Ensure handling, storage, packing, protection and delivery meet client needs and NORSOK requirements | Client/project specification; NORSOK requirements | Properly packed goods | Damage or non-conformance in transit | Consistent packing practice reduces claims |
| **B.SI.01.05** Procedure for Final Control | Project Manager | Confirm the finished product/assembly meets all requirements before release | Completed product/assembly | Release for shipment (ref. FAT) | Non-conforming product released undetected | Final control catches issues before they reach the customer |
| **B.SI.01.06** Procedure for Products Delivered by Customer | Project Manager | Protect and account for customer-owned property/material used in a project | Customer-supplied product or material | Verified, protected, returned or consumed customer property | Damage or loss of customer property creates liability and damages trust | Careful handling of customer-supplied items builds confidence in Sifab as a subcontractor |
| **D.SI.04.01 / D.SI.04.02** Handling of Nonconformities; Preventive and Corrective Actions | Project Manager / General Manager | Capture, contain and resolve nonconformities, and prevent recurrence | Reported deviation (NCR) | Closed NCR with verified corrective action | Recurring nonconformities if root cause is not addressed | Deviation trends feed continual improvement and the management review |
| **D.SI.08.01** Customer Satisfaction | General Manager | Measure and act on customer satisfaction annually | Customer survey responses | Satisfaction report to management review | Declining satisfaction goes undetected until business is lost | Early warning from survey trends enables corrective action before losing the customer |

## 3. Review

This process map is reviewed at the annual management review (**A.SI.01.01 §7.4**) and updated whenever a process changes or a new significant risk/opportunity is identified. It complements **A.SI.01.02 Context, Risks and Opportunities**, which addresses risks and opportunities at the organizational (interested-party) level, while this document addresses them at the process level.
