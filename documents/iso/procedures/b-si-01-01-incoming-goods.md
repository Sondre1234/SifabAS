## 1. Purpose

This procedure ensures that goods received by Sifab AS are checked on arrival and stored in an acceptable manner.

## 2. Scope

This procedure covers all receiving and storage of goods purchased for onward delivery to customers at Sifab AS.

## 3. Responsibility

- The **Project Manager**, or a deputy assigned to the role, follows this procedure for each delivery.
- The **General Manager** is responsible for establishing and implementing this procedure at Sifab AS.
- The **Project Manager** is responsible for proposing improvements to this procedure.

## 4. Description

When goods arrive at Sifab AS, they are placed on the shelf marked "SIFAB Varemottak" (Sifab Goods Receipt). Larger items are placed in front of the shelf.

Goods are checked against the order and inspected for any transport damage. In some cases inspection has already been carried out before the parts reach Sifab; this is communicated by the Project Manager.

Goods found nonconforming are marked "Non-Conformance" and placed in the designated area, clearly separated from the receiving area. A deviation is raised and the information forwarded; further handling follows **D.SI.04.01 Handling of Nonconformities**.

The Project Manager/Purchaser checks the documentation against the order and applicable specifications, and stores it in the relevant shared project folder. The Project Manager is responsible for following up that all parts are received. Further processing follows **D.SI.02.03 Procedure for Order Processing**.

## 5. HSE Considerations

- A Safety Data Sheet (SDS/MSDS) shall always accompany chemical shipments.
- Correct lifting equipment and technique shall be used when lifting goods, to prevent injury.

## 6. QA Considerations

When a shipment is damaged or nonconforming, a nonconformance report shall be raised per **D.SI.04.01**.

## 7. Environmental Considerations

None specific to this procedure.
