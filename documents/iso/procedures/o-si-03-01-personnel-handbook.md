## 1. Introduction

This Personnel Handbook is a reference for all Sifab AS employees. It provides a summary of company information, pay-related information, the company's disciplinary system, and information on quality management (QA) and health, safety and environment (HSE).

The Handbook is intended as a reference tool in connection with your employment and the performance of your work, in line with the company's established guidelines.

## 3. Company Description

Sifab AS was established in spring 2010. Sifab is a full-service supplier of flow metering, tank gauging, volume provers, wave monitoring, skid fabrication and special items, with project management and documentation, for the oil, gas and energy industry. Sifab builds and assembles skids at its own workshop at Bedriftsveien 20 — the physical fabrication, assembly, welding and machining work is performed by personnel rented in from qualified partners such as **Ekke Hydraulics AS**, not by Sifab's own core staff, who manage and quality-control the work. Sifab's products are subsequently installed at both offshore and onshore customer facilities, but Sifab AS itself does not operate offshore — all Sifab work is carried out from the Sandnes workshop/office or at customer premises onshore.

The company's goal is to build a solid market reputation, delivering the right product with the right quality at the right time, and to be seen by customers as a partner who solves problems efficiently and cost-effectively.

## 5. Company Policy

**Vision** — Sifab AS's business model is to build and assemble skids at its own workshop — using personnel rented in from qualified partners such as Ekke Hydraulics AS — and to source finished products and components from qualified manufacturers for project delivery and resale to customers, mainly directed at oil and gas-related activity.

**Quality** — Sifab AS aims to ensure that the products it fabricates and supplies meet the quality and standard specified by the customer, delivered on time, and that all applicable laws and regulations are complied with in production. This is achieved through continual improvement of the quality management system, including oversight of rented-in personnel and sub-suppliers' quality performance.

**HSE** — Sifab AS will actively work to continuously improve the working environment, involving all employees.

**Environment** — Sifab AS will do what is needed to protect the environment.

## 6. Sick Leave

All sick leave must be documented, either through self-certification or a doctor's sick note. When using self-certification, the employee must call in and notify company management each day of absence — a precondition for sick pay. When using a doctor's sick note, the employer must be informed on the first day of absence, and the note submitted as soon as possible thereafter.

## 7. Work Regulations

**§1 Employment** — Employees are hired by the company's General Manager or another authorized person. New employees receive an employment contract upon starting.

**§2 Working Hours** — Normal working hours are 7.5 hours/day excluding a 30-minute lunch break, from 07:00 to 15:00.

**§3 Place of Work** — The employment contract specifies the place of work. Work outside company premises should be expected for shorter or longer periods.

**§4 Pay** — Pay is compensation for hours actually worked, paid on the 20th of each month via bank transfer unless otherwise agreed. Holiday pay is paid together with June salary; half tax withholding applies to November salary. An employment agreement and confidentiality agreement are signed with new employees. Employees must verify that pay, holiday pay, travel allowances and other benefits and reimbursements are correctly calculated on receipt. Permitted deductions include statutory tax withholding and maintenance contributions, union/association deductions under written authorization, debts to the company under prior written agreement, and damages the employee has caused intentionally or through gross negligence and has acknowledged in writing or as established by judgment.

**§5 Holiday** — Unless otherwise agreed, three weeks of holiday are taken consecutively between 1 June and 30 September, with the remainder taken within the same holiday year. Holiday pay is calculated per the statutory percentage of the qualifying year's pay and is paid in full in June, per the Norwegian Holiday Act (Ferieloven) as amended.

**§6 Responsibilities and Duties** — Employees must immediately report irregularities or faults with company property to their immediate superior, and must not directly or indirectly accept personal or financial benefits from the company's business relations. The employer provides the tools necessary to perform the work. Anyone unable to attend at the agreed time must notify their immediate superior as soon as possible, stating the reason and, if possible, the expected duration. An employee absent due to illness must submit a self-certification (or doctor's note) no later than three days after the illness began; self-certified sick leave is limited to 3 days. Employees are responsible for complying with the terms of the company's insurance policies for employees, including reporting injuries and submitting claims. The employer bears responsibility and risk for work results; employees may be held liable for misuse of company equipment/materials or unauthorized withdrawal from stock. No one may appear under the influence of, or use, intoxicating substances at work, except medication prescribed by a doctor. Employees must comply with measures management deems necessary to maintain working hours. Classified documents must be handled so they cannot reach unauthorized persons; employees sign a confidentiality declaration.

**§7 Termination of Employment** — During the probationary period, a mutual 14-day notice period applies from the date of notice. For temporary employment, the mutual notice period follows the agreement. For permanent employment, the notice period is mutual per the employment contract and runs from the first day of the month following notice. Notice of termination must be given to the employee in person or by registered mail, and must state the employee's right to request negotiations and bring legal action, and the applicable deadlines, per §15 of the Working Environment Act (Arbeidsmiljøloven). The employee may terminate the agreement with the notice period stated in the employment contract; notice must be given in writing by both parties.

**§8 Dismissal** — An employee may be dismissed with immediate effect for gross breach of duty or other material breach of the employment agreement — e.g. unreasonably refusing to follow orders regarding work or workplace order; repeatedly, despite warning, failing to attend work on time or leaving the workplace without permission before the end of the working day. The decision to dismiss is made by the company's General Manager or an authorized delegate. Notice of dismissal must be given in writing, stating the employee's right to request negotiations and bring legal action per §15 of the Working Environment Act and the applicable deadlines; the reason for dismissal is given in writing on request. A dismissed employee is entitled to a reference.

**§9 Secondary Positions** — Without permission from company management, an employee may not hold another position or perform other paid work or assignments that interfere with working hours or otherwise affect their work for the company; nor undertake work where competence or insight gained through the company could benefit outsiders, where business or trade secrets could be disclosed, or where a conflict of interest could arise between the company and outside parties.

**§10 Departure** — On departure, all issued equipment, keys etc. must be returned. Oral and written authorizations cease automatically. Programs, work descriptions, documentation, data files etc. may not be taken from the company. Outstanding pay, holiday pay etc. are normally paid on the first pay day after departure, without prejudice to either party's right to raise objections where reasonable grounds exist.

**§11 Laws and Regulations** — The company and the employee are at all times bound by the applicable agreement between company and employee, the Working Environment Act, and all other applicable laws and regulations. This work regulation is established per §§10, 14 and 15 of the Working Environment Act and the Holiday Act. The Working Environment Act underlies all work at Sifab AS.

**§12 Disciplinary System** — The disciplinary system is used to ensure employees follow the guidelines given through the work regulations, customer agreements and the HSEQ system. Sifab AS applies the following disciplinary measures, in order: verbal warning (optionally in writing); warning (1st and 2nd written); final warning (written); notice of termination (written); dismissal (written). After a final warning, an employee should expect termination to follow further breaches. Warnings are counted over the preceding three years; a warning older than three years is considered expired. Serious offenses may result in immediate dismissal. Disciplinary measures are issued by the General Manager or an authorized delegate. Grounds for immediate dismissal without notice include, among others: repeated unreasonable refusal to follow safety instructions; repeated unauthorized absence despite written warnings; repeatedly disturbing workplace order; grave errors or repeated offenses endangering life, health or property; attending work intoxicated or using intoxicants at work; harassment or discrimination based on gender, sexual orientation, race, belief or cultural background; refusing mandatory fire, safety and rescue drills or safety representative instructions; misuse of communication/alarm systems; theft or fraud; breach of confidentiality; improperly seeking information about the company or other employees; taking up competing work or activity without company consent; breaching the company's HSEQ procedures and other regulations.

## 8. Quality Management

Sifab AS has implemented a quality management system to ensure good quality on all tasks performed for customers and that work is carried out as sensibly as possible. The system is a tool for continual improvement, benefiting both the company and each employee's working environment and working day. Central to this is the reporting and follow-up of improvement points and corrective actions.

### Improvement and Corrective Actions

All employees are responsible for pointing out improvement potential, errors, deviations, unwanted incidents and similar issues. Identified items are registered and described simply and concisely — ideally with specific reference to which requirement may have been breached (e.g. law, customer requirement, own or customer's QMS procedures). All reporting is done in writing on the designated form (see **D.SI.04 Deviation Handling** and the Deviations - NCR Tracker), with supporting information attached where necessary. The completed report is submitted to the employee's immediate superior, who forwards it to the General Manager and the QA/HSE lead. The General Manager (or delegate) assesses the report and any proposed corrective actions, and implements the necessary measures; preventive action to avoid recurrence is also considered and implemented where relevant. Completed corrective actions are reviewed over time by the General Manager or delegate, for suitability and effectiveness.

### 9.2 Improvement Reporting / Report of Unwanted Incident (RUH)

The procedure and form for improvement reporting and RUH under HSE is the same as described above under Quality Management — see **D.SI.04 Deviation Handling**.

### 9.3 Occupational Health Service

Sifab AS does not currently hold an agreement for a basic occupational health service package. This will be reviewed at the next management review.

### 9.4 Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

This section applies to everyone working in or entering Sifab's workshop, including personnel rented in from Ekke Hydraulics AS and any Sifab staff present.

At minimum, the following PPE must be used in Sifab's workshop and outdoor areas: helmet, where the work situation requires it; safety shoes with toe cap, except for visitors accompanied by a Sifab representative.

During work in the workshop and outdoor areas, the following PPE must also be used: flame-retardant work clothing with long sleeves and legs; eye protection (glasses/visor) and gloves during welding, cutting, grinding and similar work; hearing protection during grinding, plate cutting and other noise-risk work; eye protection where there is a risk of liquid splash (e.g. high-pressure washing, acid pickling); gloves during all work involving hot and/or sharp components/tools.

When operating Sifab's production equipment (machines/tools), PPE must be used per the user manuals, regulations, training, machine warning signage, and the requirements above.

### 9.5 Fire Instructions

- In case of fire or fire risk, immediately notify the fire brigade: **Tel. 110**
- Evacuate everyone from the building.
- Attempt to extinguish if safe to do so.
- Close doors and windows.
- Meet and inform the fire brigade on arrival.

### 9.6 Notification Plan

- In case of accident, personal injury, illness or other emergency, immediately notify AMK (medical emergency coordination): **Tel. 113**
- State who you are.
- State where you are.
- State what has happened.
- Stay on the line until told otherwise.

### 9.7 Evacuation Plan

- Leave the buildings via the nearest exit.
- Everyone gathers in the parking area for headcount.

### 9.8 Duty to Inform in Case of Accident and/or Disaster

If an employee is involved in an incident while working for the company, senior management is responsible for informing next of kin. No one other than management or their delegate may inform next of kin. In the event of a death at the workplace, the police will inform next of kin. Colleagues are informed via company management or its delegate; when the situation permits, colleagues should be proactively informed of the incident and situation.

### 9.9 Press Contact in Case of Accident and/or Disaster

No Sifab AS employee shall speak to the press. This is handled exclusively by the General Manager.

### 9.10 Post-incident Follow-up

After serious incidents, a debrief is held giving each involved person the opportunity to process their experience. Such a debrief is also offered to any injured parties and next of kin. The debrief is conducted by representatives of the occupational health service.
