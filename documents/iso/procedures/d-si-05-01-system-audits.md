## 1. Purpose

This procedure ensures Sifab AS's quality management system is audited in a planned, structured manner. Internal audits reveal non-conformities and improvement opportunities in the documented QA system. This procedure also governs Sifab's preparation for external certification audits, including the ISO 9001 recertification due **August 2026**.

## 2. Scope

This procedure covers the entirety of Sifab AS's quality assurance system, both internal audits and preparation for external audits by the certification body.

## 3. Responsibility

The General Manager is responsible for planning, carrying out and documenting internal audits. Areas of responsibility that sit directly under HSE are audited by the General Manager or a person he appoints, including external auditors where relevant.

## 4. Internal Audit Programme

- The General Manager maintains an internal audit plan covering a minimum 2-year cycle; every part of the QMS is audited at least once every 3 years. The plan is reviewed at least annually.
- **Audit notice**: involved parties receive written notice at least 1 week before an audit.
- **Conducting the audit**: the auditor prepares a detailed checklist. The audit is normally conducted as a meeting where the auditor and involved personnel discuss and review the relevant part of the documented QMS.
- Non-conformities, improvement points and comments are communicated to participants at the close of the audit.
- **Documentation**: based on the checklist and any additional notes, the auditor writes an audit report, sent to the owner of the audited documentation, and filed under **R.SI.05 Audits**.
- Non-conformities and improvement points identified are logged in the Deviations - NCR Tracker and followed up through **D.SI.04 Deviation Handling**.
- **Follow-up**: audit findings are tracked to closure through the deviation system.

## 5. External Audit Preparation (Recertification)

For the ISO 9001 recertification audit (target: August 2026), the General Manager additionally ensures:

- All documents listed in `documents/iso/master-document-list.md` are reviewed and re-issued with no open **Gap** status before the audit date.
- The Deviations - NCR Tracker reflects the current state of open/closed non-conformances, with no unresolved items lacking a corrective action plan.
- Objective evidence (records under **R.SI**) is available for each clause the auditor is expected to sample: management review minutes, internal audit reports, training records, supplier evaluations, customer satisfaction results, and calibration/FAT records.
- A pre-audit internal review is scheduled at least 4–6 weeks ahead of the certification audit to close any remaining gaps.
