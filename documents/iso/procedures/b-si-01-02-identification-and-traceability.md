## 1. Purpose

This procedure ensures that materials, components and products handled by Sifab AS are identified and traceable throughout receiving, fabrication, assembly, testing and delivery, per NS-EN ISO 9001:2015 §8.5.2. It is referenced throughout **A.SI.01.01 HSEQ Handbook §6.5** and by the other B.SI.01 technical procedures, but had no dedicated document under the pre-2026 system — this document closes that gap.

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to all materials, components, sub-assemblies and finished products passing through Sifab AS on customer projects, including items sourced from suppliers, customer-supplied property (**B.SI.01.06**), and items built into skids or packages.

## 3. Responsibility

- The **Project Manager** is responsible for defining the traceability requirements for each project, based on customer specification, NORSOK requirements, and PED where pressure-retaining parts are involved.
- **Workshop personnel** (including personnel rented from Ekke Hydraulics AS) are responsible for maintaining marking and traceability records through fabrication, under the Project Manager's/Service Engineer's oversight.
- The **Project Manager** retains traceability documentation in the project folder.

## 4. Description

### 4.1 Identification at Receipt

On receipt (**B.SI.01.01**), material and components are checked against the purchase order and marked or tagged with a reference that ties the item to its purchase order, material certificate, and (where applicable) heat/batch number.

### 4.2 Traceability Through Fabrication

Where a project requires traceability (per customer specification, NORSOK M-101/M-650 or equivalent, or PED Category-driven requirements), Sifab maintains:

- Material certificates (e.g. EN 10204 3.1/3.2) linked to the relevant purchase order and project.
- Records of which welder, welding procedure (WPS/WPQR) and heat number were used, where welding is performed at Sifab's own workshop by personnel rented from Ekke Hydraulics AS or an equivalent partner, or subcontracted to an external fabricator.
- Serial or unique identification numbers for instruments, metering skids and pressure-retaining assemblies, carried from receipt through fabrication to final test and delivery documentation.

### 4.3 Marking

Physical marking (tags, stamps, or labels) is applied so that an item's identity is not lost during handling, storage or assembly (ref. **B.SI.01.04**). Where a customer specifies a marking convention, that convention is followed in place of Sifab's default tagging.

### 4.4 Traceability at Delivery

Traceability records (material certificates, test/calibration reports, FAT records) are compiled into the delivery documentation package per **B.SI.01.05 Procedure for Final Control**, and retained in the project folder for the retention period defined in **D.SI.06 Document Control**.

### 4.5 Loss of Traceability

If identification or traceability is lost or compromised for an item, this is treated as a nonconformity per **D.SI.04.01 Handling of Nonconformities**.

## 5. HSE Considerations

Correct identification of pressure-retaining parts and their material certificates is a safety-critical control; loss of traceability on such items shall always be raised as a deviation.
