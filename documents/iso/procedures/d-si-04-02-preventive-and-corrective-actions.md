## Purpose

This procedure is established to:

1. Carry out preventive actions.
2. Verify that corrective actions are implemented and effective.
3. Carry out and register the resulting changes to procedures.

## Definitions

None.

## Scope

This procedure applies to all of Sifab AS.

## Responsibility

The General Manager is responsible for following, establishing, implementing and reviewing this procedure.

## Description

Corrective and preventive action is a routine and important part of management and meeting activity at Sifab AS. Actions that are planned but cannot be implemented immediately are entered into an action plan. Corrective and preventive actions are planned and carried out so the intended effect is achieved, considered in context so other processes are not adversely affected.

### Corrective Actions

Corrective action means identifying the cause of a specific problem and implementing the action needed to prevent recurrence. Corrective action may be triggered by:

- Customer complaints
- Customer satisfaction levels
- Supplier problems
- Nonconformities / RUH reports (**D.SI.04.01**)
- Audit findings
- Rework or repairs
- Training evaluation
- Production disruption
- Delayed delivery
- Waiting time

Decisions on the activities that make up the corrective action, and a completion date, are recorded on the meeting-minutes form. Where the corrective action originates from a nonconformity or RUH, the relevant minutes are referenced on the nonconformity report.

Major corrective actions require General Manager approval before implementation. After a suitable interval, the General Manager confirms the action had the intended effect; the timing and result of this check are recorded alongside the earlier records (minutes, report, measurement, etc.). The General Manager reviews the records periodically to confirm the procedure is functioning as intended.

### Preventive Actions

Preventive action exists to stop potential problems from negatively affecting business results, products, processes, the quality management system, or customer satisfaction, and to help ensure HSE is maintained. Preventive action may be based on:

- Recommended maintenance intervals for equipment
- Monitoring of machine capacity
- Market and customer research
- Staff/personnel challenges
- Personnel feedback on HSE
- Sales trends

A PPM (preventive maintenance) meeting can, in some contexts, count as a preventive activity.

Where a potential problem is identified and preventive action is warranted, it is registered, carried out and followed up using the same process as for corrective actions. Sifab makes the resulting changes to procedures and documents following corrective and preventive action decisions.
