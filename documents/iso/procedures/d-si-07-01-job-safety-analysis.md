## 1. Purpose

This procedure describes the use of Job Safety Analysis (JSA) at Sifab AS. The aim of a JSA is to identify and evaluate hazards employees are exposed to when performing work activities, and to identify and implement the measures needed to carry out the job as safely as possible.

## 2. Definitions

**JSA** — Job Safety Analysis.

## 3. Scope

This procedure applies to all Sifab AS projects. The Project Manager/foreman must continuously evaluate the need for a JSA before starting any new task in a project.

The need for a JSA is evaluated based on:
- The task's complexity.
- Whether the work method is known/unknown (not covered by existing procedures).
- Experienced/inexperienced personnel.
- Safety risk in the task.

If the Project Manager is in doubt about the need for a formal JSA, they must consult their superior.

A JSA is always required for:
- Work that deviates from existing procedures, or where there is no prior experience.
- Complicated lifting operations.
- Work where safety representatives or involved personnel require a JSA.
- Work where the customer requires a JSA, documented in the test and inspection plan.

## 4. Responsibility

The Project Manager is responsible for ensuring all work is carried out as safely as possible. In job planning, they must confirm necessary preventive measures are planned and implemented, and that a JSA is carried out where required.

The Project Manager/foreman is responsible for carrying out the JSA, ensuring identified measures are implemented, that the responsible person signs for them, and that everyone involved is informed. Personnel carrying out the work must ensure it is done safely and follow the measures identified in the JSA.

## 5. Description

### 5.1 Preparation and Execution

- The Project Manager is responsible for setting up the JSA form and involving everyone concerned in the analysis.
- Particular emphasis is placed on HSE measures to prevent harm to personnel and the environment. When a potential hazard is identified, action is taken to minimize the hazard and a person is assigned responsibility for it.
- Once the JSA form is completed, the Project Manager ensures everyone involved has understood the content and their role, and signs the form.
- The JSA form must be accessible to personnel during the work (posted, or distributed to each employee).
- If personnel are replaced before the work is complete, the new personnel must be informed of the JSA content.

## 6. HSE Considerations

Follow agreed communication routines. Ensure open and safe communication between everyone involved.

## 8. Attachments

**S.SI.01 — JSA Form**, reproduced below for reference (the controlled form itself is filed under S SSI - Forms).

> **Job Safety Analysis**
>
> A Job Safety Analysis is used when the work meets at least one of the following criteria:
> - The work is defined as risky and no other risk analysis has been carried out or established.
> - The work is defined as risky and the work description deviates from the standard procedure.
> - The work is defined as risky and is performed rarely / for the first time.
>
> The JSA is reviewed by everyone involved before work starts.
>
> This Job Safety Analysis applies to (operation): _______________________
> Carried out by (persons): _______________________ Date: _______________
>
