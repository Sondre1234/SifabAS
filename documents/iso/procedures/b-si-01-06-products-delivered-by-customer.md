## 1. Purpose

This procedure ensures that customer-owned property is not damaged or lost while in Sifab AS's care. It ensures no damage occurs to customer products, that they are handled in accordance with Sifab's receiving procedure, and — where these items are built into larger assemblies — that Sifab requests the necessary documentation and, where appropriate, acts as the customer's goods receipt point.

## 2. Definitions

**PO** — Purchase Order.

## 3. Scope

This procedure applies to all products, documents and information delivered to Sifab AS by its customers.

## 4. Responsibility

- The **Project Manager** performs goods receipt and storage of products delivered by the customer.
- Every Sifab AS employee who receives information, a PO, or other documents from a Sifab customer is responsible for safeguarding these so they are not damaged or reach unauthorized hands. Packing slips are filed in a binder; relevant documentation is scanned and stored in the corresponding shared project folder.

## 5. Description

The Project Manager receives the customer's property/products, marks them against the relevant PO, and ensures they are stored safely.

The Project Manager ensures that all parts, machines, etc. delivered by the customer are uniquely marked/tagged (ref. **B.SI.01.02 Procedure for Identification and Traceability**).

If customer property or products are damaged — including transport damage — Sifab AS raises a deviation and informs the customer as soon as possible (**D.SI.04.01**).

## 6. HSE Considerations

All storage of customer-supplied property must be carried out safely.

## 7. QA Considerations

None beyond the deviation-handling reference above.

## 8. Environmental Considerations

A Safety Data Sheet (SDS/MSDS) shall accompany all chemical deliveries.
