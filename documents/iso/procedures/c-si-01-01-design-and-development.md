## 1. Purpose

This procedure defines how Sifab AS performs design and development work to fulfil customer, governmental and standards requirements. Design and development (ISO 9001:2015 §8.3) is a **permitted exclusion** from Sifab's in-house scope per **A.SI.01.01 HSEQ Handbook §1.4**: Sifab does not carry out design engineering itself, but manages the design and development process on behalf of the customer and purchases the design work from approved sub-suppliers. This procedure governs that managed process — planning, review, verification and validation of the subcontracted design — so Sifab retains full control of design quality and compliance even though the design work itself is outsourced.

## 2. Scope

This procedure covers all design and development activity carried out for Sifab AS projects, whether performed by an approved sub-supplier or, exceptionally, in-house, from initial planning through construction release.

## 4. Responsibility and Authority

The Project Manager (PM) is responsible for ensuring this procedure is followed during execution of design and development, and for defining who may design, check and approve design work for each discipline. Where design is subcontracted, the PM is responsible for selecting an approved supplier (per **D.SI.01 Purchasing and Supplier Approval**), and for sending and receiving all required design documentation.

## 5. Procedure

### 5.1 General

Design and development is normally outsourced to a Sifab-approved supplier; it may exceptionally be performed internally where Sifab holds the relevant competence. In both cases, the process follows the principle of gated review — defined approval steps between each phase.

### 5.2 Planning

Design and development planning begins within the quotation and contract review process (**D.SI.02 Quotation, Contract and Order Handling**), where customer requirements are defined and agreed. Where a customer's requirements or quote do not align with applicable regulations or standards, Sifab has a duty to advise the customer accordingly, based on its own standards knowledge.

Design input to be considered includes:
- **Customer requirements**: function, layout, material choice, cost versus function, coating, HSE.
- **Governmental and standards requirements**: the design must be checked against applicable regulations and referenced standards (NORSOK, PED, etc.); these must be available to, and known by, the engineer responsible.

Design work starts only once the goal and scope are agreed and understood between Sifab and the customer, and — where subcontracted — once a Purchase Order has been issued to the design supplier following contract review. Any change in scope between the quote and the PO is handled through the contract review process before design work begins.

### 5.3 Design Review

An initial design review evaluates customer specification, applicable standards and regulations, and sets out the plan for the design work. A design review checklist is used for this evaluation. The PM is in charge of the design review but may delegate; the PM includes authorities and the customer in the review where needed.

Design tools: Sifab's approved suppliers typically use AutoCAD or Inventor, as these are widely used in the industry and support electronic sharing of files and documents.

Document control during design:
- Documents carry a standard title bar, review bar and comment bar.
- Documents always reference a revision number for identification and traceability.
- The **Prepared** field is signed by the designer/drafter on first issue.
- The **Checked** field is signed by the person appointed by the PM.
- The **Approved** field is signed by the PM (or, where agreed with the customer, confirmed by e-mail — including for 3D models used as a customer presentation tool). Other approval formats may be agreed with the customer.

### 5.4 Design, Output and Internal Verification

Design output includes drawings, tables, calculations, process data, and bills of material. Once the design engineer confirms the output complies with the design input and applicable regulations, the document proceeds through the sign-off process (§5.3).

Depending on design complexity, an interdisciplinary check (IDC) may be required before submission. After internal verification, drawings and documents are submitted to the customer marked **Issued for Comments** or **Issued for Approval** (IFA), signed Prepared / Checked / Approved as above.

### 5.5 Review of Customer Feedback

When feedback or approval is received from the customer, it is reviewed and, if required, the design is revised per §5.3–5.4. Once approved, drawings are updated and re-issued **Issued for Construction (IFC)**.

### 5.6 Release to Construction

IFC drawings and specifications are submitted to the project for the next actions per the project plan — purchasing of parts, manufacturing, assembly, etc.

### 5.7 Changes to an Approved Design

There shall be no changes to an approved design without customer approval. Where the customer changes the input information used for an approved design, a Variation Order Request (VOR) is raised and considered before any re-design work starts; the design process then restarts from §5.2/§5.3 as applicable.

### 5.8 Verification and Validation of Design and Development

**Verification** confirms the design meets the specified design input, performed as required during design review; verification evidence includes a fully-signed review bar, minutes of meeting (MOM) held with the customer, or e-mail approval. Designs requiring third-party certification are verified and stamped by the relevant third party before design approval. For assemblies, parts are compared to confirm assembly is achievable.

**Validation** confirms the completed design is capable of meeting the specified requirements in use. Validation methods include physical testing of the design, pressure testing, Factory Acceptance Testing (FAT), or computer simulation. All verification and validation activity is assessed against defined acceptance criteria and signed off.

### 5.9 Records

All records created during design and development are stored per **D.SI.06 Control of Documents**, and per any additional project-specific requirements.
