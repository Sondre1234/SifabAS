## 1. Purpose

This document identifies Sifab AS's internal and external interested parties, their requirements and expectations, and the associated risks and opportunities, per ISO 9001:2015 §4.1, §4.2 and §6.1. It is reviewed annually alongside the Sifab AS Strategy document and the management review.

---

## 2. Internal and External Interested Parties

| Interested Party | Requirements / Expectations | Risks | Opportunities | Actions / Response |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Owners** | Profitability, growth | — | Good financial oversight | Ongoing financial status logged per project |
| **Competitors** | — (market pressure) | Losing market share to competing products; technological disadvantage; higher pricing eroding position | Gain market share; win new customers with improved/new products | Actively engage with suppliers/partners to improve products; analyze competitors; leverage recent product successes (ref. Sifab AS Strategy, SWOT) |
| **Customers, Regulators, Employees, Suppliers, Owners/Shareholders** (climate & ESG) | Effective risk management related to climate change; transparent ESG reporting | Rising energy and raw material costs; extreme weather disrupting logistics/production; new reporting and emission requirements; pressure from customers/regulators/investors on environmental responsibility | Increased customer satisfaction, regulatory compliance and reputation from demonstrating environmental responsibility | Monitor emerging ESG/reporting requirements; factor into strategy review |
| **Customers** | Delivery per specification; on-time delivery; regular meetings and follow-up; quality and competence (ref. **D.SI.08 Customer Satisfaction**) | Missing payments; limited company size; unhappy customers | New customers; increased sales to existing customers; green energy / CO2, H2, ammonia-related projects | Credit-check new customers before approval; proactive customer communication; email campaigns |
| **Suppliers / Partners** (ref. **D.SI.01.03 Evaluation and Approval of Suppliers**) | Monitor technology trends; understand product requirements; align supplier products with market expectations | Products not to spec/requirements; products becoming outdated versus competition; lost sales from unhappy clients | Up-to-date products; increased sales; new product developments | Regular status meetings with suppliers; annual meetings with main partners |
| **Employees** (ref. **D.SI.02.03 Procedure for Order Processing**; Arbeidsmiljøloven) | Working environment; personal development; job content; adherence to QA system; involvement in QA/HSE work | Staff turnover; lack of teamwork; underperformance | Employee involvement in processes; team building; strengthened sales team; training | Investment in employees; Monday meetings; team-based ways of working |
| **Market** (ref. Sifab AS Strategy) | Understanding of market size, requirements and constraints; market research and planning | Cultural issues; declining market; reduced client investment | Read market demand; identify trends early | Market planning; seminar attendance; networking; new product lines (e.g. Faure Herman USM) support new market share |
| **Authorities / NORSOK** | Deliver per specification; product responsibility for pressurized equipment | Failure to meet specification | Qualified-supplier status | Purchase and maintain current NORSOK standards; conformity assessment (Samsvarsvurdering) |
| **ISO 9001:2015** | Certification as evidence of quality | Oil companies require certified suppliers | Continual improvement via PDCA; learning from deviations | Focus on deviation reporting and follow-up; maintain continual improvement |
| **Facilities** | Offices, workshop, systems | New workshop area outgrowing capacity | Proximity to partners supports cooperation | Schedule regular meetings with partners; monitor facility capacity against order book |
| **Social and Economic Environment** | — | High interest rates raising cost base; slow market reducing demand; recession | High unemployment can enable hiring skilled staff at competitive cost; slow market may open room for new product lines | Build customer network; stay ready for new opportunities; track new supplier products |
| **Currency Exchange Rates** | Purchase and sell in matching currency where possible | Losses from adverse currency movement | Gains from favorable currency movement | Evaluate currency exposure per project; sell USD/EUR when rates are favorable |

---

## 3. Review

This document is reviewed annually as part of the Sifab AS Strategy process and the management review (**A.SI.01.01 §7.4**), and updated whenever a material change occurs to an interested party's requirements, or a new significant risk or opportunity is identified.
