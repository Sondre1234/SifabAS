## 1. Introduction and Scope

### 1.1 Intention

This HSEQ Handbook gives an overview of the structure of Sifab AS's documented management system and how the system is used. It functions as the company's overall QA Plan, addressing the risks and opportunities associated with Sifab's context, objectives and processes. The purpose of the document collection referred to as the "quality management system" is to control all work that affects quality, to ensure the desired outcome of our processes.

### 1.2 Scope

This Handbook describes the documentation of Sifab's quality and HSE systems. It is a comprehensive system description and also serves as evidence to customers of Sifab's ability to deliver the required quality. The system is tailored to Sifab AS and is embedded in the total system surrounding all of the company's activities.

### 1.3 Distribution

Sifab's management system — this Handbook, procedures, processes and forms — is available to all employees in electronic format. The Handbook may be distributed to customers, suppliers and other interested parties by request.

### 1.4 Permitted Exclusions

**Clause 8.3, Design and Development** — Sifab AS purchases technical design work from sub-suppliers; Sifab employees work mainly as project managers. Design and development is therefore addressed through supplier control (D.SI.01) and the design procedure (C.SI.01.01) rather than as an in-house design function.

### 1.5 Normative Reference

NS-EN ISO 9001:2015, Quality management systems — Requirements.

### 1.6 Terms and Definitions

Terms and definitions follow NS-EN-ISO 9000:2015 and the Norwegian Internal Control Regulation ("Forskrift om internkontroll").

### 2.2 Organization, Interested Parties and Context

Sifab's organization, interested parties, risks, opportunities and context are addressed in a dedicated document — see **A.SI.01.02 Context, Risks and Opportunities**. Sifab holds an annual strategy review covering market, risk, opportunities, objectives, resources, budget and product range.

### 2.3 Scope of the Quality Management System

The General Manager is responsible for the QA system. Implementation of QA policy is a management responsibility. Active use of the system contributes to profitability, competitiveness, quality and company culture.

Sifab's main activity is agency sales, project management, and fabrication/assembly of skid packages of limited size and complexity (up to approx. 3 × 3 m) at its own workshop, using personnel rented in from qualified partners such as Ekke Hydraulics AS rather than employing fabricators directly. Sifab does not operate in-house design and development, or hold control/measurement equipment; where projects have special requirements, the project manager is responsible for sourcing the necessary equipment or capability from a sub-supplier.

### 2.4 QA System and Its Processes

The quality system is scaled to Sifab's organization and personnel resources, structured so processes and procedures can be located quickly for day-to-day use. The QA system, HSEQ Handbook and processes are available to all employees in Zigma360.

## 3. Leadership (ISO 9001 §5)

### 3.1 Leadership and Commitment

Management is responsible for:
- Ensuring the QA system is used effectively.
- Ensuring QA policy and objectives are established and aligned with Sifab's context and strategy.
- Ensuring the QA system's requirements and intent are implemented in the organization's business processes.
- Promoting process approach and risk-based thinking, providing necessary resources, and ensuring the system delivers results.
- Maintaining the organization chart (**O.SI.01 Organization Chart**) and position descriptions (**O.SI.02 Job Descriptions**) that define each role's responsibility and authority.

Responsibility for HSEQ is a line responsibility — a formal and practical responsibility for every employee in their daily work. Everyone in the organization follows the management system in all their activities, ensuring work is carried out safely and that quality is maintained. Anyone leading or controlling the work of others is responsible for safety and environment during that work, without diminishing each individual's own safety responsibility.

### 3.2 Customer Focus

Sifab ensures customer requirements and expectations are established and documented in agreements, orders and specifications, and met with the goal of achieving customer satisfaction. Complaints or comments from customers are treated as valuable information for improvement. Customer satisfaction is monitored per **D.SI.08 Customer Satisfaction**.

### 3.3 Policy

Sifab AS has established a Quality Policy (**A.SI.02.01**) and an HSE Policy (**A.SI.02.02**). Both are communicated to all employees, displayed at strategic locations in the company, and the signed Quality Policy is published on www.sifab.no. Both policies are reviewed during the management review to confirm they still reflect the company and its objectives.

## 6. Operation (ISO 9001 §8)

### 6.1 Operational Planning and Control

Procedures and processes control acceptance and the resources needed to achieve conformity with product and service requirements. Procedures for product control and receiving inspection ensure products are delivered in accordance with customer requirements.

### 6.2 Requirements for Products and Services

Sifab maintains regular customer communication during the sales phase using SuperOffice CRM; dialogue and customer actions are logged and followed up there. Customer communications are stored securely on OneDrive, and Zigma360 is used to register inquiries and quotations before they are sent to the customer.

Contract review uses a checklist at a contract review meeting to confirm customer, statutory and internal requirements are met (**D.SI.02 Quotation, Contract and Order Handling**). Orders below NOK 500,000 within Sifab's established product range may omit the formal contract review meeting. The completed checklist is stored in the project folder.

### 6.3 Design and Development (§8.3)

Sifab primarily manages design and development on behalf of customers and purchases design services from sub-suppliers; planning of these activities follows the contract review and order process. See **C.SI.01.01 Procedure for Design and Development**, which covers design inputs, outputs, changes, verification and validation even where the design work itself is subcontracted.

### 6.4 Control of Externally Provided Processes, Products and Services (§8.4)

All critical suppliers are pre-approved before purchasing, and evaluated on performance annually, per **D.SI.01.01 Procedure for Purchasing** and **D.SI.01.03 Evaluation and Approval of Suppliers**. Purchase orders specify the requirements for the product to be supplied, based on customer specifications, drawings and standards. Incoming goods are subject to receiving inspection (**B.SI.01.01 Procedure for Incoming Goods**) checking quantity, specification and other critical parameters against the order.

### 6.5 Production and Service Provision (§8.5)

Production and service provision is organized by project. Fabrication and assembly of skids takes place at Sifab's own workshop, using personnel rented in from qualified partners such as Ekke Hydraulics AS, under Sifab's own quality system. Each project has a QA plan describing how in-process control will be carried out (**B.SI.01.03 Procedure for Control and Testing**). Sifab validates its production and service processes by evaluating completed projects, tracking non-conformances, and conducting customer satisfaction surveys.

- **Identification and traceability**: applied through receiving, fabrication, assembly and delivery, where required by the project (**B.SI.01.02**, referenced in technical procedures).
- **Customer property**: stored per **B.SI.01.06 Procedure for Products Delivered by Customer**.
- **Preservation**: goods are stored per **B.SI.01.04 Procedure for Handling, Storage, Packing and Shipment**; special preservation requirements may be specified by the customer.
- **Post-delivery activity**: Sifab offers service work and spare parts after delivery and follows up for customer feedback.
- **Control of changes**: changes are documented in a Variation Order Request (VOR), stating price and delivery consequence, and sent to the customer for approval before work proceeds.

### 6.6 Release of Products and Services (§8.6)

Sifab follows project routines for release of products for shipment; a Factory Acceptance Test (FAT) is normally performed before shipment. FAT and calibration reports are stored in the project folder.

### 6.7 Control of Nonconforming Outputs (§8.7)

Nonconforming products identified at receiving inspection or elsewhere are identified and controlled to prevent unintended use or delivery. The customer is informed as required, and deviations are logged and followed up per **D.SI.04 Deviation Handling**, tracked through the Deviations - NCR Tracker.

## 7. Performance Evaluation (ISO 9001 §9)

### 7.1 Monitoring, Measurement, Analysis and Evaluation

Sifab monitors process performance through the deviation system and internal meetings/communication, identifying which procedures generate the most deviations and where action is needed. System effectiveness and suitability are assessed at the management review.

### 7.2 Customer Satisfaction

Customer satisfaction is measured annually (Q4) via questionnaire or in customer meetings, per **D.SI.08.01 Customer Satisfaction**. Results are reported at the management review; low scores trigger a follow-up meeting with the customer.

### 7.3 Internal Audit

Internal system audits are conducted on management's initiative to confirm the QA system remains appropriate, effective and complied with. Different parts of the system are audited as needed (minimum once every 3 years), per an annual plan approved by the General Manager (**D.SI.05 Audit**).

### 7.4 Management Review

Management review is carried out at least once a year, attended at minimum by the General Manager (or delegate) and the HSEQ lead, with detailed minutes recorded (**R.SI.03 Meetings / R.SI.04 Plans**). The review covers, at minimum:

- Status of actions from the previous management review.
- Whether the HSEQ management system meets current standards, laws and regulations.
- Achievement of last year's objectives, and reasons for any shortfall.
- Objectives for the coming year.
- Findings and follow-up from internal and external audits.
- Deviations and their resolution, and whether corrective actions worked as intended.
- Status of certification/accreditation (including the ISO 9001 recertification).
- Working-environment survey results.
- Review of critical suppliers.
- Review of the HSEQ system for purpose, usability, and improvement opportunities.
- Training completed and planned.
- Strategy for the coming year.
- Customer satisfaction survey results.

## 8. Improvement (ISO 9001 §10)

### 8.1 General

Sifab considers continual improvement of internal processes and management systems, in step with market and system changes, essential to the business.

### 8.2 Nonconformity and Corrective Action

Corrective actions are planned and implemented following deviation handling, audits or reviews, and followed up to confirm they meet their purpose (**D.SI.04.02 Procedure for Preventive and Corrective Actions**). Preventive actions are identified from trends in recorded deviations, customer satisfaction results and internal audit findings, and discussed at appropriate meetings. Documentation of both is retained in Zigma360.

### 8.3 Continual Improvement

Sifab maintains continual focus on improving the suitability, adequacy and effectiveness of the quality management system.
