# NACE MR0175 / ISO 15156 — Sour Service Material Requirements

## Scope

NACE MR0175 / ISO 15156 (three-part standard, ISO 15156:2020 5th edition) defines requirements for selecting and qualifying metallic materials in oil and gas production environments containing H2S. Part 1 covers general principles, Part 2 covers carbon and low-alloy steels, and Part 3 covers corrosion-resistant alloys (CRAs). For Sifab AS, this standard applies to all wetted metallic materials in metering skids, prover vessels, and special items where the process fluid is classified as sour based on H2S partial pressure thresholds in Part 1 §5.

## Key Requirements for Sifab AS

- **Sour service threshold**: H2S partial pressure > 0.3 kPa (0.05 psi) in the presence of free water triggers compliance. Severity regions 0–3 (Part 2, Annex B) determine the level of material restriction. Sifab must obtain fluid composition, temperature, and pH data from the operator to confirm classification before issuing a material specification.
- **Hardness limits — carbon and low-alloy steel (Part 2 §6)**: Maximum 22 HRC (250 HV10 / 237 HBW) for base metal, weld metal, and HAZ. Vickers HV10 hardness surveys on production welds are required, covering weld metal, fusion line, and HAZ at 1 mm and 3 mm from the fusion line.
- **PWHT**: Recommended in the base standard; made mandatory for carbon steel by Equinor TR2000 regardless of wall thickness. Sifab must apply PWHT on Equinor projects.
- **Bolting**: Standard ASTM A193 B7 / A194 2H are not acceptable in sour service. Acceptable alternatives: A193 B7M / A194 2HM and other grades listed in Part 2 Annex A.
- **Duplex and super duplex stainless steel (Part 3)**: Must be in solution-annealed condition with verified ferrite content (30–70%). Environmental limits (temperature, chloride, H2S partial pressure) apply per Part 3 Annex A tables. Maximum hardness 28 HRC (22Cr) or 32 HRC (25Cr super duplex).
- **SSC testing**: Per NACE TM0177 (Method A tensile or Method D four-point bend) when material is not pre-qualified in Annex A, or when the operator requires it. Required by Equinor TR2000 on many Equinor projects.
- **HIC testing**: Per NACE TM0284 for plate and pipe in Region 2 and Region 3. Equinor TR2000 frequently mandates HIC testing for all sour service plate and pipe regardless of severity region.
- **Material certificates**: EN 10204 Type 3.1 minimum; Type 3.2 required by Equinor TR2000. Certificates must include hardness results; SSC/HIC test reports must be attached where required.
- **Elastomers and seals**: Not covered by this standard — reference NORSOK M-710 for elastomer and seal qualification in H2S service.

## SifabAS Agent Usage

- **Primary**: Metering Agent — qualification of all wetted materials (prover vessels, flow meters, manifolds, valves) for sour service on NCS projects
- **Primary**: Structural Agent — material selection for skid piping and structure, WPS hardness compliance, and production weld hardness survey planning
- **Supporting**: Process Agent — sour service classification based on fluid composition and partial pressure data; input to the material selection report
- **Supporting**: Quality Management Agent — material certificate review for hardness compliance; SSC/HIC test report inclusion in the project documentation package per B.SI.01.07
