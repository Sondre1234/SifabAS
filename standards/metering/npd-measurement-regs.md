# Måleforskriften / NPD Measurement Regulations — Norwegian Fiscal Metering Requirements

## Scope

The Norwegian Measurement Regulations (FOR-2001-11-01-1234, commonly called "Måleforskriften") establish the legal requirements for measurement of petroleum (oil, gas, condensate, and NGL) produced on the Norwegian Continental Shelf for fiscal purposes, allocation, and CO2 tax calculation. The regulations define required measurement uncertainty, system design principles, proving requirements, flow computer standards, sampling requirements, and reporting obligations to the NPD. Sifab AS delivers fiscal and allocation metering systems and volume provers that operators use to comply with these regulations; every such system must be designed to meet the requirements below.

## Key Requirements for Sifab AS

- **Fiscal oil metering uncertainty (§5)**: Combined measurement uncertainty must not exceed ±0.30% of standard volume at 95% confidence level. Sifab's oil metering skid designs — including flow meter, density measurement, temperature, water content, and prover — must achieve this in the uncertainty budget.
- **Fiscal gas metering uncertainty (§6)**: Combined measurement uncertainty must not exceed ±1.0% of standard volume (energy) at 95% confidence. Covers flow meter, pressure, temperature, and gas composition instrumentation.
- **Allocation metering (§9)**: Relaxed uncertainty requirements compared to fiscal, but a formal uncertainty analysis is still required. Proving requirements apply. Sifab delivers both fiscal and allocation metering systems.
- **Proving requirements (§10)**: Volume provers must be used to verify and calibrate flow meters at defined intervals (fiscal: minimum quarterly). Prover base volume uncertainty must be significantly better than the meter being proved (typically ±0.02% for pipe provers, ±0.02–0.05% for SVPs). Prover repeatability: ≤±0.02% over five consecutive runs (pipe prover); ≤±0.05% (SVP). Sifab's volume provers are the primary tool enabling operators to meet this requirement.
- **Meter types**: The regulations accept turbine meters, positive displacement meters, Coriolis meters, and ultrasonic meters — provided they are installed per API MPMS Chapter 5 and the uncertainty budget is met. Meter selection and straight-length requirements must comply with API MPMS Ch. 5 and NORSOK I-104.
- **Sampling (§11)**: Representative sampling of process fluid is required for density, water content, and composition analysis. Sampling systems must comply with ISO 3171 / API MPMS Chapter 8. Sifab integrates sampling systems on metering skids as part of the full package.
- **Flow computers and calculations (§12)**: Flow computers must perform calculations per API MPMS Chapter 11 (volume correction factors) and Chapter 12 (petroleum quantity calculations). Full audit trail, data security, and tamper detection are required per API MPMS Chapter 21.
- **Calibration traceability (§14)**: All measurement instruments must be calibrated with traceability to national standards. Calibration certificates must be maintained. Proving intervals and re-proving triggers (e.g., post-maintenance) must be documented in the metering study.
- **Documentation package (§16)**: Operators must maintain metering system design documentation, uncertainty analyses, calibration records, and proving records. Sifab provides the as-built documentation package as the foundation for this requirement.
- **NPD approval (§18)**: Fiscal metering system designs must be formally approved by the NPD before installation. The operator submits a metering study to NPD; Sifab provides the technical documentation (P&IDs, data sheets, uncertainty analysis, prover sizing) to support the submission.
